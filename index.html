Abstract

Human interpretation of the world encompasses the useof symbols to categorize sensory inputs. Recent methodshave successfully been able to decompose and disentanglevideo sequences into their composing objects and dynamics, in a self-supervised fashion. However, there has been scarceeffort in giving interpretability to the dynamics of the scene. We propose a method to decompose a video into moving ob-jects and their attributes, and model each objectâ€™s dynamicswith linear system identification tools, by means of a Koop-man embedding. This allows interpretation, manipulation,and extrapolation of the dynamics of the different objectsby employing the Koopman operatorK. We test our methodin various synthetic datasets and successfully forecast chal-lenging trajectories while interpreting them.
